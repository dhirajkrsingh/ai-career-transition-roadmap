# Research-Backed Learning Resources

This file captures public learning paths and why they matter for AI career switchers.

## Google Machine Learning Crash Course

Why it matters:

1. practical foundation in regression, classification, metrics, and overfitting
2. strong emphasis on data handling and production ML systems
3. includes refreshed material on embeddings and LLM basics

Good for:

- people who need ML fundamentals without a huge upfront barrier

## fast.ai Practical Deep Learning For Coders

Why it matters:

1. optimized for people who can code and want to learn by building
2. covers deep learning applications, deployment, and practical workflows
3. explicitly lowers the intimidation barrier around math-first learning

Good for:

- software engineers and builders who learn best by doing projects

## Full Stack Deep Learning

Why it matters:

1. covers the full AI product lifecycle, not just models
2. emphasizes tooling, testing, data management, deployment, monitoring, and LLMOps
3. aligns closely with real-world applied AI roles

Good for:

- people targeting AI engineering, ML engineering, or productized AI systems

## Hugging Face Learn

Why it matters:

1. offers focused tracks for LLMs, agents, deep RL, and open-source AI tools
2. useful for understanding modern open-source workflows and ecosystem tooling
3. helps learners move from using APIs to understanding model ecosystems

Good for:

- learners aiming at applied LLM, agent, or open-source AI work

## DeepLearning.AI: AI For Everyone

Why it matters:

1. strong orientation material for understanding AI workflows and limits
2. useful for product, business, and cross-functional transitions
3. helps role switchers understand team structures and realistic AI project selection

Good for:

- non-technical or partially technical professionals entering the AI space

## roadmap.sh AI Engineer Roadmap

Why it matters:

1. reflects the current applied AI tooling landscape
2. connects prompting, RAG, agents, safety, models, and infrastructure topics
3. useful as a breadth map for self-assessment

Good for:

- learners who need a visual map of what the field contains

## How To Use These Resources Together

Recommended path:

1. Start with orientation material if your role target is unclear.
2. Build ML foundations with Google ML Crash Course.
3. Build practical deep learning skill with fast.ai or similar hands-on work.
4. Move into product and systems thinking with Full Stack Deep Learning.
5. Add modern LLM and agent skills through Hugging Face and applied project work.
6. Use roadmap.sh as a breadth checklist, not as your only curriculum.
