# AI Switch Readiness Checklist

You are closer to being ready when most of these are true.

## Foundations

- I can code in Python without constant hand-holding.
- I understand basic ML terms and evaluation metrics.
- I can explain overfitting, generalization, and train/test split.
- I understand the basics of neural networks and LLMs.

## Applied Ability

- I have built at least one AI-related project end to end.
- I can explain how the system was evaluated.
- I can discuss limitations and failure modes honestly.
- I can choose when AI is appropriate and when it is not.

## Production And Reliability

- I understand the basics of deployment, monitoring, or operational trade-offs.
- I can talk about hallucinations, grounding, or model limitations.
- I can explain cost, latency, privacy, or safety concerns at a practical level.

## Career Positioning

- My GitHub or portfolio shows real AI work, not only course notes.
- My resume reflects AI-relevant outcomes.
- I can tell a coherent story about why I am moving into AI and what I already bring from my current background.

## Decision Rule

If you cannot yet show proof of build ability and evaluation discipline, you are still in learning mode, not yet in strong transition mode.
