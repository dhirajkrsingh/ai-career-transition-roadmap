# Module 6: From Any Role To AI

## The Core Idea

You do not switch into AI by pretending your previous career did not happen.

You switch by combining:

1. what you already know deeply
2. what AI teams currently need
3. what you can learn fast enough to become useful

## Bridge Formula

Use this formula:

**Current strengths + AI foundations + visible proof of work = credible transition path**

## If You Come From A Technical Role

Your main job is usually not to learn how to code.

Your main job is to learn:

1. data and model behavior
2. evaluation discipline
3. AI product constraints
4. how unreliable outputs fail in practice

## If You Come From A Non-Technical Role

Your main job is not to become an expert theoretician first.

Your main job is to build enough technical fluency to:

1. understand the workflow
2. build small working systems
3. evaluate outputs critically
4. collaborate with technical teams without hand-waving

## What A Real Bridge Looks Like

### Example: Product Manager -> AI PM

Do not just take AI strategy courses.

Also do:

1. one small build project
2. one evaluation framework exercise
3. one case study showing how you scoped an AI feature and measured success

### Example: QA -> AI Evaluation Engineer

Do not try to imitate a research scientist.

Also do:

1. learn Python
2. build prompt test suites
3. create benchmark cases for hallucinations and regressions
4. show how your QA mindset improves AI system quality

### Example: Software Engineer -> AI Engineer

Do not stop at API usage.

Also do:

1. learn model behavior and evaluation
2. build one grounded AI application
3. document trade-offs in retrieval, prompting, latency, and cost

## General Rule

The most credible transition narrative is not:

"I want to work in AI because AI is the future."

It is:

"I already know how to solve these kinds of problems, and I have now learned how to solve them using modern AI systems with measurable quality."
