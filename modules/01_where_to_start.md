# Module 1: Where To Start If You Want To Switch Into AI

## The First Mistake Most People Make

They ask, "What AI course should I start with?"

That is usually the wrong first question.

The right first question is:

**Which AI role am I trying to move into, and what parts of my current background already transfer?**

## Common AI Role Targets

| Role | What They Usually Do |
|------|----------------------|
| **AI Engineer** | Build AI-powered applications, LLM systems, agents, and production pipelines |
| **ML Engineer** | Train, deploy, optimize, and maintain ML models and data pipelines |
| **Data Scientist** | Analyze data, build predictive models, communicate insights |
| **MLOps Engineer** | Productionize models, manage infra, monitoring, CI/CD, reproducibility |
| **AI Product Manager** | Define AI product opportunities, requirements, metrics, and roadmap |
| **Applied Research / Research Engineer** | Push model quality with experiments, papers, and deeper technical work |

## Decision Rule

Use this to narrow your path:

1. If you like building products and shipping systems, target **AI Engineer**.
2. If you like models, metrics, and experimentation, target **ML Engineer** or **Data Scientist**.
3. If you like infrastructure, reliability, and tooling, target **MLOps Engineer**.
4. If you like user problems and product strategy, target **AI Product Manager**.

## Minimum Starting Questions

Before learning, answer these honestly:

1. Can I code confidently in Python?
2. Can I work with data and notebooks?
3. Do I understand basic statistics and evaluation?
4. Am I targeting product building, model building, or AI strategy?
5. What proof of work can I realistically build in 90 days?

## If You Have No Clear Direction

Start with the AI Engineer path.

Why:

1. It is broad enough to expose you to prompting, APIs, evaluation, and productization.
2. It aligns with current industry demand for applied AI builders.
3. It gives faster visible outputs than starting with pure research tracks.

## What Not To Do

Do not start by trying to read research papers end-to-end if you cannot yet build a small AI project.

Do not start with advanced math if you do not yet understand the workflow of an ML or LLM application.

Do not confuse using AI tools with understanding AI systems.
